Setting Up Your Service Accounts

A simple, step-by-step guide to creating the accounts that power your app — so payments, messages, and emails all run under your own name.

Prepared by Achraf Benamrane · Developer | Progix | GetDraft

Hi! 👋 To launch GetDraft under your own name, a few services need to run on your accounts — so payments go to your bank, text-message and verification costs are billed to you, and emails are sent from your brand.

You don't need to be technical. Just follow the steps, copy a few codes, and send them to us safely (see the last page). If you get stuck on any step, take a screenshot and send it to us — we'll point you to the exact spot.

⏱️ Total time: ~30–45 minutes ☕ Tip: do them one at a time
📌 Before you start: create every account with your company email (e.g. you@yourcompany.com), not a personal one — so the business owns everything. Use a strong password and save it.

The 4 accounts we need

1

💳 Stripe — to accept payments

What it's for: lets users pay for subscriptions and extra swipes in the app. The money goes straight to your bank account.

  1. Go to Create a Stripe account → enter email, name, password → confirm the email Stripe sends you.
  2. Fill in your business details and bank account when asked (this is how you get paid). You can skip this for now and finish it before going live — testing works without it.
  3. Get your keys at Developers → API keys: copy the Publishable key (pk_…) and click “Reveal” to copy the Secret key (sk_…).
  4. Create your plans at Product catalogue → + Add product → make one per plan (e.g. Starter, Pro) with a monthly price → Save. Open each and copy its Price ID (price_…).
📩 Send us: Publishable key (pk_…), Secret key (sk_…), and each plan's Price ID (price_…). (We'll set up the payment webhook for you — nothing to do there.)
2

📱 Twilio — to send SMS codes

What it's for: when a user signs up with a phone number, Twilio texts them the verification code. The small per-message cost is billed to you.

  1. Go to Sign up for Twilio → create the account → verify your own email + phone.
  2. Upgrade from the free trial (add a payment method). ⚠️ Important — on the trial, Twilio can only text numbers you've manually verified; upgrading lets it text all your users.
  3. On the Twilio Console home, in the “Account Info” box, copy the Account SID (AC…) and the Auth Token (click to reveal).
  4. Create a verification service at Verify → Services → Create new → name it “GetDraft” → copy the Service SID (VA…).
📩 Send us: Account SID (AC…), Auth Token, and Verify Service SID (VA…).
3

✉️ Resend — to send emails

What it's for: sends email verification codes and notifications from your own domain (e.g. noreply@yourcompany.com) so they look professional and reach inboxes.

  1. Go to Sign up for Resend and create your account.
  2. Add your domain at Domains → Add Domain → type your domain. Resend shows a few DNS records.
    🛠️ This is the one slightly technical step — just send us a screenshot of those records (or give your website/IT person access) and we'll help you add them. ~5 minutes.
  3. Create the key at API Keys → Create API Key → name it “GetDraft” → copy it (re_…).
📩 Send us: the API key (re_…), your domain, and the “from” address you want (e.g. GetDraft <noreply@yourcompany.com>).
4

🪪 Didit — to verify identities (KYC)

What it's for: checks a user's ID document + face during signup, so you know real people are on the platform.

  1. Go to Create a Didit business account.
  2. In your dashboard, open Workflows (or “Verification”) → find the KYC workflow → copy its Workflow ID.
  3. Open Settings / API → copy your API Key.
  4. Open Webhooks → copy the Webhook Secret (signing secret).
💡 Didit's menus can vary — if you can't find any of these three, send us a screenshot of your dashboard and we'll point to the exact spot.
📩 Send us: the API Key, the Workflow ID, and the Webhook Secret.

Optional — only when you're ready to publish

📲 App-store accounts

Not “keys,” but needed to put the app on the stores under your business name. You can do these later.

  • Google Play (Android) — one-time $25 → Google Play Console sign-up
  • Apple (iPhone) — $99/year → Apple Developer Program

Your app's database and server hosting are managed by our team for now — we can transfer them to your own accounts whenever you'd like.

Please read — sending the keys safely

🔒 How to send us the keys safely

These keys are like passwords to your money and accounts — never paste them in a normal WhatsApp or email message.

  • Easiest safe way: create a free Bitwarden account → make a Secure Note → paste all the keys → use “Send” to share it with us.
  • Or use a secure link we set up together — just ask and we'll send one.
  • ⚠️ If you ever share a key in a normal chat by accident, tell us — we'll have you regenerate it (it's quick).

Final checklist — what to send us

Stripe: Publishable key (pk_…), Secret key (sk_…), Plan Price IDs (price_…)
Twilio: Account SID (AC…), Auth Token, Verify Service SID (VA…)
Resend: API key (re_…), your domain, your “from” email
Didit: API Key, Workflow ID, Webhook Secret
Later: Google Play + Apple Developer accounts

Once we have these, GetDraft runs 100% on your accounts — your payments, your messages, your brand. 🚀
Stuck on any step? Send us a screenshot — we'll guide you through it.
